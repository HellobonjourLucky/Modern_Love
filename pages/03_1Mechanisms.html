Most people think that the advent of smart devices cause many problems. But I believe the mechanisms embedded within social networking applications are primarily responsible for social problems, such as the obsession of digital self-presentation and many addictive problems. Particularly in Instagram, TikTok, or Tinder, these platforms offer highly satisfying personal experiences that entice users to spend extended periods of time using their services.

To deliver this personalized experience, social networking platforms gather extensive data, and users often provide their personal data and preferences without much consideration. Then, algorithms process all user data to enhance the service and offer more personalized experiences to users. Consequently, algorithms have become powerful tools for driving automated decisions to provide highly satisfying personalized experiences, despite the simple definition of algorithms as a procedure for solving computational problems with mathematics.28
