Given the impact of digital media on our lives and relationships, a fundamental psychological inquiry concerns digital self-presentation and society.15 I think an important source to study the significance of digital self-presentation and its impact on societal relationships are social networking platforms.

On these platforms, self-representation involves individuals utilizing diverse communication modes, such as text and image, to show their identities in online settings. Particularly on online dating platforms, individuals employ various methods of self-presentation to create an attractive digital persona aimed at appealing to potential partners.

But the obsession of digital self-presentation could negatively impact mental health.16 Because it can cause anxiety disorder and depression during the establishment of online relationships, as many individuals invest a significant portion of their lifetime in crafting an ideal online presentation of their narrative selves to garner maximum "likes" on social media platforms or dating applications.17
